Task 1: Photocell Extension Wires

A photocell, also known as a light-dependent resistor (LDR), is a variable resistor that senses light. Sometimes components such as sensors need to be placed away from the breadboard or circuit board. This exercise will show you how to solder extension wires to the photocell.

Materials and Tools

  • 1 photocell
  • 2 lengths of 22 AWG hookup wire (cut from the spools)
  • helping hands
  • soldering iron
  • solder
  • heat gun

Photocell

1A. Strip the ends of the wire so that each piece has one longer exposed end (for the light sensor) and one shorter (for the breadboard).

1B. Wrap the photocell legs securely around the two longer wire ends. Hold in place with the helping hands.

1C. Solder the connection between the wire and photocell legs.

1D. Cover the exposed metal areas with heat shrink tubing. Use a heat gun to shrink it. Your connections are now insulated!
