As we move past the halfway point in the semester, it is important to revisit and adjust your project plan. This document covers both a critical audit of your V1 plan and a revised plan for the remainder of the semester.

Part 1 - Project Audit

Go back to your V1 plan and critically assess your current progress in relationship to where you had proposed in V1.

  • What has gone right?
    What aspects of the project are evolving as you planned them - conceptually, technically, or as an overall production.
  • What has not gone to plan?
    Things always change when you get a project underway and it is important to assess what hasn't gone to plan at this stage. In addition to noting it, you should assess how critical this is. How big of a deal is it that it didn't go to plan?
  • What are interesting things you have discovered?
    New ideas and concepts often come out as you actually start to create the work. What are interesting things you have discovered that were not in the original plan?
Part 2 - Project Plan V2

Considering both your project audit and your V1 plan, create a new version for now through GradEx.

  • Critical Must Haves
    What are the 1 - 3 most critical things to finish with your work. List them in order of importance. Everyone's work scales in different ways, but it is important to note: What do you HAVE to get sorted out? How far off are you from getting there?
  • OK -> Excellent
    What are the key things that will take your project from being OK to Excellent. List 2 - 3 factors and put them in order of importance.
  • Timeline
    Weekly development plan. If you are working on a collaborative project be sure to list each team member's tasks and deliverables individually.
  • Key Milestones
    • PlayTest 2
      What are the key aspects of the work that you plan to test in the second playtest. This isn't just about what stage you want the project to be at. Consider which aspects would benefit most from tester input at this stage.
    • Final Course Presentation
      Taking into account all of the factors in Part 2 - note where you think the project will be for the final presentation. Be ambitious but honest with yourself.
    • GradEx
      If you plan to participate in GradEx, provide an overview of where you want the project to be. Consider the specific opportunities and challenges for the event:
      • How do you deal with the scale?
      • How do you explain your work to a public that has no idea what you are doing?
      • What additional materials do you need to create? Not everyone wants to actually interact with work, but how can you provide an experience where they can also engage more passively?
      • How will your project work when you are not there?
