Considerations for Gradex

And for exhibiting interactive work in general

Overview

Give your work the largest 'surface area' possible

1 Consider Multiple Angles About Your Work

Your project can fit into several different categories - technical, experiential, visual, etc.

Create media that shows it from these angles. This provides more hooks for people to engage with.

2 Multiple Ways to Engage With the Work

This is an extension of point 1. Not everyone wants to actually interact with interactive work - provide ways for people to engage that aren't just 'playing'.

This is also important because sometimes a person only has a few minutes. Don't lose them because of this. Have the 'quick version' of your project someone can understand.

This can include video trailers, printed material, and clear text.

3 Plan for When You Are / Aren't There

Never be OFF. Seeing interactive work that is off is disappointing.

However, you are not expected to be with the work the entire time. Have a clear video that can run on your screen when you aren't there.

Make a clear plan around key times.

4 Have a Clear Way for People to Share Your Work
  • Buy a clear URL. It's worth the money and immediately makes your work more legitimate.
  • Have a clean/simple website that explains the work, tells more about the people who made it, and how to contact them.
