LEDs are light-emitting diodes. In order to work, they must be connected in the correct orientation. Here are some diagrams to help you identify which side is positive and which side is negative:

Breadboards & Resistor NON-Polarity

Unlike LEDs, resistors Do Not have a polarity. This means that it does not matter which end goes on which side of the circuit. They are often oriented in the same way for visual clarity, but this does not affect how the circuit performs.

Breadboards & Wiring

You have two different connector types included in your kit. Jumpers and pre-cut wires

Jumpers

Jumpers are flexible, fixed length connectors that allow you to quickly make connections on your breadboard. Their advantage is that they are quick to implement. Their disadvantage is that they can get messy because of their length. Jumper wires are only used on breadboards and shouldn't be soldered onto protoboards.

Pre-cut wires

The pre-cut wires are simply solid core wire that has been cut and stripped at a set length. These can be used on both breadboards and protoboards to create a cleaner profile of the circuit. Also, these can be cut to custom lengths to match the needs of your circuit.

Colours and organization

Regardless of whether you are using jumpers or wires, colour-coding is important. While the colour does not change the properties of the wire/jumper, it does provide you with a means to visually organize your circuit. There are many approaches, but it is important to be consistent as this will benefit you when coding and debugging your circuit.

A few standard conventions

  • Black : Ground
  • Red : Power

Step 5: Create the circuit on a protoboard.

Create a protoboard circuit based on the circuit illustration in Step 2.

Solder the headers first. Prepare the headers by following these instructions: Prepare Socket Headers to attach the Arduino to the Protoboard

Then do the remaining components, one at a time. Be sure to double check your placement of components before soldering.

Here are some assembly tips:

Be sure to add the sockets from your kit to the protoboard.

DO NOT SOLDER YOUR ARDUINO DIRECTLY TO THE BOARD

Use 2 X 15 pin sockets from your kit

Tape can be used to hold components in place when soldering.

The legs of components can also be bent to provide a secure fit.

Bending the legs of components such as resistors at a right angle can help them to sit flat on the protoboard.

When you are done, be sure to trim any extra wire on the back of the circuit board with a pair of small snips.
