Workshop 1 - Real Things, Fake Project

Design an interactive installation for the entry of 205 Richmond - don't actually build it, but show how you would.

Duration: 3 weeks

Format: Groups of 3 (randomly assigned)

Description

Workshop 1 asks you to imagine and design a new interactive experience for the entry lobby of 205 Richmond that changes the way people currently use the space. The design must incorporate a screen or many screens and some form of interactivity1. The goal is to design the space using products available today, but with no limitation on the budget. You do not need to actually program the media or interaction, but you do need to produce drawings, images, videos that explain how it COULD work.

Constraints

  • The design can engage any surface or space within the entry lobby. Walls, floor, doors, windows, stairs
  • The boundary is set by: the front doors, the elevator doors, the doors to xfab, and the doors to the 1st floor. See boundary details
  • While your proposal in no way needs to be practical, you should consider how it would overlay with the existing functions of the space

Tools

  • Photoshop
  • fSpy
  • 3d modelling software (Blender/Maya)
  • Drawing (Paper or Screen)

Files & Links

Workshop 1 Steps

  1. Find your Group
  2. Create a system for how you will share files and communicate
  3. Determine your location
  4. Develop multiple concepts for that location
  5. Decide on one to move forward with
  6. Set the roles within your team – people can have more than one, but be sure it is spread evenly
  7. Create a first version of a visualization of the design with a list of associated components
  8. Meet as a team to refine the design and plan for the final version
  9. Create the final visualizations, budget, and narrative
  10. Create a way to clearly present the work and capture feedback
  11. Make final refinements based on the feedback you receive
  12. Create the pdf of the documentation

Deliverables

September 23 - Final Documentation Submission

Documentation
  • Description: 100 – 200 words that describe the concept of the project and how it works
  • Visualizations: 2-3 images that show how the experience would look in the space
  • Diagrams: 1-2 drawings or diagrams that explain how the system works
  • Component Budget Breakdown: Total cost estimate in CAD with item breakdown including description, price per unit, quantity, and supplier link
  • Optional: Mock-up video showing the interaction and effects
  • Format: Single PDF file with optional video links to Vimeo/Youtube

1 What is/is not considered a screen or screen-based is intentionally open to your interpretation. Focus on proposing why your concept fits these guidelines instead of asking for permission
